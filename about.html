Why The Bachelors of Science in Biology?

I was previously a pre-med student and have actually had extensive experience in the medical field. I was registered with the Oregon Board of Nursing as a Certified Nursing Assistant shortly after graduating highschool. I then went and got my B.S in Biology with a Minor in Chemistry. I graduated Summa Cum Laude and began teaching at the OSU Cadaver Lab for a year. After my year at the lab I scribed for a year and a half before applying to medical school. Before submitting my application, I decided to switch careers due to irreconcilliable moral differences with the field. Medicine is amazing, the profession is full of wonderful people, unfortunately it just wasn't for me.

Why Computer Science?

From a young age I had alot of exposure to technology from my parents. I got my first email at the age of 8, a hotmail. My parents both encouraged technological literacy as they were both computer engineers at intel. I have always been interested in not only how computers function, but the science of computing and solving complex or interesting problems. I made a very difficult decision to study Biology over Computer Science when first entering college. For better or for worse, I am now returning to a familiar passion and starting a career in computer science!
